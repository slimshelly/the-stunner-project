The first song I fell in love with.

Alex Yang

27 Jul 2018

Lost cause by Beck is probably the first song I fell in love with. I first heard it through the game: beyond: two souls, where it was covered by Ellen Page. The point in the game where Ellen's character was on the run from what I could remember, she crosses a guitarist in the winter, where it was too cold to play, so she asked if she could give it a go.

Despite having one of the most uneventful and weird music videos I've ever seen, beck's smooth voice made this song timeless for me.

This song itself is part of my coming of age, learning the guitar, singing along late at night.

I listen to too many love songs, that's probably a bad thing. I guess this song is about someone trying to breakup with someone they no longer love, the other person is a lost cause, he or she is pitied upon, but he or she is not a good person, so no one cares, and despite the singer caring for the person in the past, the singer is now sick of this person's bullshit, and knows that although no one else will take care of this person, he is choosing to let go and move on.

That was really badly written, but I think it's pretty straight forward. Although it is a breakup song, I feel like it is really delicately worded, it has a protective warmth, a gentleness that's spilling out, like the break up had to happen, for everyone's sake.

Your sorry eyes, they cut through the bone, they make it hard, to leave you alone.

Baby you're a lost cause.
